# What is a Swarm City Token (SWT)?
<p dir="ltr"><span style="font-size: large;">The Swarm City Token (SWT) is the ONLY unit of exchange on the Swarm City platform.&nbsp;SWT are <a href="https://theethereum.wiki/w/index.php/ERC20_Token_Standard">ERC-20</a> tokens that are essentially smart contracts that interact with the Ethereum blockchain.</span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span style="font-size: large;">All ARC tokens were minted during the November 2016 ITO and the majority of ARC have already been converted to SWT. There will be no extra tokens minted. </span></p>
<p dir="ltr"><span style="font-size: large;">A total of 9,525,397 ARC tokens were minted, so at most only 9,525,397 SWT can ever be minted. This number includes the 16% tokens minted in addition to the ARC Token Sale and these are exchangeable as well. </span></p>

<h2>The token contract</h2>
<p dir="ltr"><span style="font-size: large;">The SWT token contract is based on the <a href="https://github.com/swarmcity/minime">MiniMe</a> token contract created by Griff Green and Jordi Baylina. The MiniMe token contract is a state-of-the-art ERC20 compatible token contract that provides some extra features:</span></p>
<ul>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: large;">Balance history is registered and available to be queried</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: large;">All MiniMe Tokens maintain a history of the balance changes that occur during each block. Two calls are introduced to read the totalSupply and the balance of any address at any block in the past.</span></li>
</ul>
</ul>
<p dir="ltr"><span style="font-size: large;">View the Swarm City token contract with <a href="https://etherscan.io/token/SwarmCity">Etherscan</a>&nbsp;or <a href="https://ethplorer.io/address/0xb9e7f8568e08d5659f5d29c4997173d84cdf2607#pageSize=100">Ethplorer</a>.</span></p>

# Can I store SWT on my Trezor?
<p><span style="font-size: large;">Yes, SWT can be held in any <a href="https://swarmcitysupport.groovehq.com/knowledge_base/topics/what-is-swarm-city-token-swt?from_search=true">ERC-20</a> token compatible wallet, including the Trezor hardware wallet. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: large;">When holding cryptocurrency you have 2 basic options, either protect your funds or have someone else do it. When you depend on someone else to protect your tokens, you must trust a 3rd party to protect your data and your tokens. With a hardware or paper wallet, you must trust yourself not to lose the private keys or seeds. Hardware wallets are more secure because your private key never leaves the hardware wallet.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: large;">At this time, there is no centralized wallet, like Coinbase, that is compatible with SWT, so if you want to hold SWT, you must first learn how to use a digital wallet, how to send/receive cryptocurrency, how to protect yourself, how to acquire SWT, etc.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: large;">MyCrypto or MEW and Trezor collaborated to work with ERC-20 tokens, which includes SWT.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></p>

<p><span style="font-size: large;">Once you have familiarized yourself with your Trezor device and have secured the seeds, you are ready to use it to hold your SWT.</span></p>
